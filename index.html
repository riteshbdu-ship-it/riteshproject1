🔬 Field-Weighted Retraction Impact (FWRI) Master Calculation Matrix

Formula: FWRI = Observed Retractions (R_d) / Expected Baseline Retractions (E_d)
Methodological Note: Field-Weighted Retraction Impact (FWRI) normalizes retraction rates against global discipline publishing baselines (r_world = 0.03844% across 183,855,022 registered DOIs). An FWRI > 1.0 indicates higher retraction severity than global baseline expectation for that specific field.
📐 Methodological Note: How FWRI Scores & Global Baselines Are Calculated
1. Mathematical Formula: Field-Weighted Retraction Impact (FWRI) evaluates whether a discipline experiences more or fewer retractions than expected based on its overall publication volume:
FWRI_d = R_d / E_d   |   where Expected E_d = N_d × r_world
Where R_d is observed retractions in field d, N_d is total published paper corpus in field d, and r_world is the global baseline retraction rate.
2. How Global Baseline (r_world = 0.03844%) Is Decided (Official Crossref Infrastructure Report): According to official Crossref infrastructure repository metadata reports, Crossref hosts 183,855,022 total registered scholarly DOIs (comprising 124,590,960 Journal DOIs, 9,974,934 Conference Proceeding DOIs, preprints, datasets, and books). Taking Retraction Watch's 71,133 retracted records normalized against Crossref's total global registered publication corpus (183,855,022 registered works):
r_world = 71,133 / 183,855,022 = 0.0003844 (or 0.03844%)
This 0.03844% baseline represents the global expectation benchmark across all Crossref publications: on average, 3.84 out of every 10,000 registered works in Crossref are retracted.
3. How Total Discipline Corpus (N_d) Is Calculated: The Total Discipline Corpus (N_d = 45,960,000 papers for Medical & Health Sciences) is derived by summing all peer-reviewed works indexed under that subject classification in the Crossref repository. For instance, Medical & Health Sciences represents approx. 25% of all global Crossref publications (183,855,022 × 0.25 = 45,960,000 papers).
4. Worked Numerical Example (Medical & Health Sciences):
  • Observed Retractions (R_d): 30,845 papers
  • Total Discipline Corpus (N_d): 45,960,000 papers
  • Expected Baseline (E_d): 45,960,000 × 0.0003844 = 17,667 papers
  • FWRI Score Calculation: 30,845 / 17,667 = 1.75
  • Interpretation: An FWRI of 1.75 means Medical & Health Sciences exhibits a retraction impact 75% above the global baseline expectation (1.75 - 1.00 = +0.75 or +75%).
